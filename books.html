~/books

Books that have shaped my thinking and approach to mathematics, finance & computer science

The Creativity Code: Art and Innovation in the Age of AI

by Marcus du Sautoy

AI & Art

This book I read is really interesting. It's about how Gen AI evolved since a decade into being able to create art, that we sometimes interpret as human.

Link Here

A Practical Guide To Quantitative Finance Interviews

by Xinfeng Zhou

Brain Teasers & Maths Puzzles

This book is nearly 20 years old, but it’s amazing. It contains many really interesting puzzles. I don’t use it only for interviews but also because it’s fun.

Link Here

Mathématiques financières, 3ème édition

by Pierre Devolder

Mathematics for Finance

This French book is amazing. It has taught me a lot about the basics of finance. It's the best introduction to quantitative finance.

Link Here

Les Maths en tête - Algèbre

by Xavier Gourdon

Algebra & Probabilities

This French book has been with me for 2 years of preparatory classes, and I often read it because it’s full of great problems.

Link Here

A Mathematician's Lament

by Paul Lockhart

Mathematics Education

I read this book in 2021, and I recommand it because it argues that mathematics are a form of art, and should be taught in a way that inspires people.

Link Here

Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives

by John C. Hull

Finance & Derivatives

This book does not need an introduction. It’s the best book on derivatives. I am currently reading it.
